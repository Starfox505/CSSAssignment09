What worked well for you over the last 9 weeks that aided in your learning process?

I think the thing that helped me most was the amount of help and support I got from the professor. I was extremely overwhelmed with both this class and the Javascript class, but he helped tremendously in keeping me on track! I think overall the thing that helped me the most is communication, as there was a lot of it throughout the course with the class and also one on one.

What would you change about the course?

I would personally say that the only thing I would change to the course is changing the manner in which we do our Lecture Activities. While they are extremely important, and really do set you up for the assignments, I feel that they should be done in class, instead of at home before class. I loved the workshops, but I feel that the Activities would help others who are confused on the assignments or the concepts. Therefore, I would suggest to do the Lecture Activities as soon as you come in to class, and then immediately after do the homework workshop.

Did you learn what you expected in this course?

I definitely did learn exactly what I was expecting to learn in this course, as I was ultimately hoping to learn typography and color immensely. While I did not do great in those two realms, I felt I got a much better grasp on how important both of these things are to web design. Overall I would highly suggest the class to anyone interested in web design.

What concepts were new to you and what concepts had you already encountered in the past?

I would say a lot of the concepts I was aware of and encountered previously in HTML. However, the concepts were taken into much great depth than before! Therefore, I was unaware of how much CSS can do, and really how much there is to learn. I felt that this class definitely is best taught separate from the HTML/CSS course, as the basis that is given in the HTML/CSS class is great but not as in-depth on CSS and the immense possibilities that CSS has to offer.
